 Reducing Diesel Dependency in Isolated Labrador Communities

Administering body: Natural Resources Canada (NRCan), in partnership with Indigenous Services Canada (ISC) and the Government of Newfoundland and Labrador.
Source URL: https://www.canada.ca/en/natural-resources-canada.html (search: "Reducing Diesel Dependency Labrador")
Total fund: $220 million (announced in Budget 2022 over multiple years).
Per-project max: Not publicly fixed; project allocations decided in partnership with the Innu Nation, Nunatsiavut Government, and NunatuKavut Community Council.
Geographic scope: Labrador only. The 20 isolated diesel communities along the Labrador coast and central Labrador are the program's target population.

 Who is eligible

- Communities in Labrador currently dependent on diesel for primary electricity generation.
- Projects led by, co-led with, or in formal partnership with the Innu Nation, the Nunatsiavut Government, or the NunatuKavut Community Council.
- Utility-led projects (e.g., Newfoundland and Labrador Hydro) where the community has signed off on the proposal.

 What's funded

- Capital costs for renewable generation (wind, solar) and battery storage in isolated systems.
- Pre-feasibility and feasibility studies.
- Capacity building for Indigenous community energy planning and project management.
- Backup diesel retention costs where required for reliability.

 Application timing

- Rolling intake through partnership tables (no fixed annual deadline).
- Projects typically negotiated through multi-year agreements rather than single grants.

 Key caveats

- Not available outside Labrador. Island outport communities (Francois, McCallum, La Poile) cannot apply to this program.
- Indigenous priority is structural, not just preferential — non-Indigenous applicants need an explicit Indigenous partnership.
- Funding is sequenced against the 2030 federal target to get all remote Indigenous communities onto clean power.
- Project ownership and revenue sharing terms vary; consultation with the relevant Indigenous government is required before any application.
