 Indigenous Off-Diesel Initiative (IODI)

Administering body: Natural Resources Canada (NRCan) with Indigenous Services Canada (ISC) and Crown-Indigenous Relations and Northern Affairs Canada (CIRNAC).
Source URL: https://natural-resources.canada.ca/our-natural-resources/energy-sources-distribution/clean-energy/funding-clean-energy-projects/indigenous-off-diesel-initiative
Total fund: Approximately $300 million across multiple streams.
Per-project max: Varies by stream — capacity-building cohorts typically $300k–$5M per community; deployment grants up to ~$25M per project.
Geographic scope: National. Indigenous-led projects in remote (off-grid or partial-grid) communities anywhere in Canada.

 Who is eligible

- Indigenous Nations, governments, communities, and Indigenous-owned businesses leading clean energy and demand-side projects.
- Non-Indigenous proponents with a documented Indigenous partnership (community is the lead beneficiary).
- The community must be currently dependent on diesel for primary electricity.

 What's funded

- Energy Champion cohort (capacity building, two-year placements within communities).
- Pre-development costs: feasibility studies, community engagement, environmental assessment prep.
- Deployment of renewable generation, storage, and demand-side measures (efficiency, heat pumps).
- Community-led energy planning and IRP development.

 Application timing

- Cohort intake runs roughly every 18–24 months. Deployment funding is awarded through invitation following cohort completion.
- Outside the cohort cycle, projects can sometimes be funded through bilateral agreements with NRCan / ISC.

 Key caveats

- Community ownership is structural. Projects without Indigenous-led governance of the asset (or a clearly negotiated path to it) are weaker fits.
- The capacity-building stream and the deployment stream serve different communities — moving from one to the other is not automatic.
- IODI prioritises holistic transition plans (generation + storage + efficiency) over single-asset proposals.
- Funding may be combined with provincial programs and other federal sources, but stacking rules apply.
