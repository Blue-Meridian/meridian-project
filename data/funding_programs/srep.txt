 Smart Renewables and Electrification Pathways (SREPs)

Administering body: Natural Resources Canada (NRCan).
Source URL: https://natural-resources.canada.ca/our-natural-resources/energy-sources-distribution/clean-energy/funding-clean-energy-projects/smart-renewables-and-electrification-pathways-program
Total fund: Approximately $2.2 billion over multiple years (expanded under Budget 2023).
Per-project max: Project-specific; typical capital contributions are tens of millions for utility-scale projects.
Geographic scope: National. Designed for utility-scale and grid-modernisation projects, applicable to large off-grid systems where scale is sufficient.

 Who is eligible

- Utilities, system operators, independent power producers.
- Indigenous-led and Indigenous-partnered projects (dedicated stream).
- Large project consortia working on grid modernisation, smart grids, transmission, or substantial renewable generation.

 What's funded

- Utility-scale wind, solar, storage, run-of-river hydro, geothermal.
- Grid modernisation (smart-grid hardware, advanced metering, distribution upgrades).
- Transmission projects supporting renewables interconnection.
- Critical regional electrification infrastructure.
- For remote / off-grid: applies when the project is large enough to be treated as a regional system rather than a single-village microgrid.

 Application timing

- Multiple intake rounds per year; specific deadlines published by NRCan.
- Indigenous stream has separate intake windows.

 Key caveats

- Project scale matters. Smaller community microgrids (capital < ~$5M) are typically a poor fit. SREP is most relevant when projects are large enough to look like utility-scale assets, or when bundled across multiple communities.
- Applicants must demonstrate the project's contribution to grid reliability, GHG reductions, and electrification goals.
- Indigenous stream offers higher cost-share ratios and dedicated capacity support compared to the general stream.
- Stacking with other federal programs (IODI, CERRC) is allowed within program-specific caps; teams should plan stacking up front rather than retrofit.
- For Meridian: applies to bundled multi-community proposals (e.g., regional microgrid procurement covering 5+ Labrador communities) or to the largest single-community projects (Nain class).
